My Coding Journey 9/12/16

In January 2016, I was facing a minor crisis in my life. I had gone back to school and had been taking classes for 2 years. I was about to apply to nursing school when it hit me: I didn't want to be a nurse. I was 28, had a Bachelor's degree in music, a full 2 years of nursing classes, and I was back at square one. As I often do in times of trouble I went to lunch. I called my friend and told him my problem. He asked me "what do you want to do?" I told him that I wanted a career where I had to use my brain to solve problems, where I could build something and make it exactly how I pictured it in my head. He suggested I look into web development. That was 9 months ago and it was the best suggestion I could have recieved.

I dove head first into coding. That day I switched my nursing classes for computer science classes. I researched all the different paths one could take to learn how to code. I settled on FreeCodeCamp.com. I chose them because they had a very indepth curriculum to becoming a full stack web developer and a great community to help whenever you needed it.

Thanks to the countless hours I spent on FreeCodeCamp.com, Stack Exchange, and MDN I was able to build this very website and all the content in my work section. Thanks to that one simple suggestion I found a profession that I'm excited to do. I love being able to turn an idea into a reality and look back on it and say that I built that from scratch. I even love the frustrating, yelling at your computer at 3 in the morning parts. And the best part is that there is so much to learn, I'll never run out of topics. Learning how to code has been one of the best things I've ever done and I can't wait to see what comes next.
